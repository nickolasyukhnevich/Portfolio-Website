Case Study

NY Personal Logo Identity

A geometric monogram that fuses “N” and “Y” into a flexible brand mark.

This project focuses on building a clean personal logo system for my portfolio and digital presence. The mark needed to be simple enough for a favicon, but strong enough to anchor layouts, thumbnails, and motion graphics.

Project Details

  • Role: Brand Designer
  • Tools: Illustrator, Photoshop
  • Focus: Logo system, scalability, visual consistency

Final Logo Lockup

The finished NY monogram in primary color treatment.

Process & Progress

From rough sketch to refined vector system.

01 — Sketch Exploration

I began with quick pencil sketches exploring how N and Y could overlap, share strokes, or mirror each other while keeping the mark readable.

02 — Grid & Geometry

I then moved into vector, using a simple grid to align angles and stroke weights. This stage focused on balance and symmetry so the logo feels stable at any size.

03 — Application & Testing

By showcasing the logo on physical merchandise, this mockup reinforces the scalability and commercial readiness of the design. It proves the monogram can support a full brand ecosystem, from UI layouts to clothing, tote bags, packaging, and other promotional materials.
